Period at work: plan your day with comfort and confidence

Periods can affect meetings, commuting, uniforms, long shifts and focus. This guide helps visitors prepare for workdays without shame or panic.

Workday planBuild a kit

Work essentials

  • Keep products in your work bag.
  • Know bathroom access and breaks.
  • Track symptoms before demanding days.

Prepare before leaving home

Check your expected cycle date, pack supplies, and choose clothing that makes you feel secure. For long shifts, bring more products than you think you need. If you wear a uniform, keep a spare item if possible.

Meetings, commuting and long shifts

Use breaks strategically on heavy days. If meetings are long, change products beforehand when possible. During commuting, keep a small pouch where it is easy to reach. A spare bag for stained clothing can prevent stress.

Confidence and privacy

You do not need to explain your period to everyone. Privacy is valid. At the same time, having one trusted colleague, manager or friend who can help in an emergency may reduce anxiety.

FAQ

What should I keep at work?

Keep preferred products, spare underwear if possible, wipes or tissues, and a small disposal or laundry bag.

How do I manage cramps during work?

Use safe comfort habits such as heat when available, hydration, rest during breaks and gentle movement. Severe pain should be checked.

What if I leak at work?

Change calmly, use spare clothing if available, and ask a trusted person for help if needed. Leaks are common and not shameful.
