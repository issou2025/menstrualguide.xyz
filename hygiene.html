Menstrual Hygiene Guide

Essential tips for staying clean, comfortable, and healthy during your period

Why Menstrual Hygiene Matters

Good menstrual hygiene is essential for your health, comfort, and confidence. Proper care prevents infections, reduces odor, and helps you feel fresh throughout your cycle.

Remember

Your period is natural and nothing to be ashamed of. Taking care of yourself during menstruation is an important part of overall health and self-care.

Change your menstrual products regularly to prevent bacterial growth and odor:

  • Pads: Every 4-6 hours (or sooner if saturated)
  • Tampons: Every 4-8 hours (never exceed 8 hours)
  • Menstrual cups: Every 12 hours
  • Period underwear: Follow manufacturer guidelines

Clean your genital area properly during your period:

  • Use warm water and mild, unscented soap
  • Wash from front to back to prevent infections
  • Clean the external area only - avoid douching
  • Pat dry with a clean towel

Always maintain clean hands:

  • Wash hands before and after changing products
  • Use soap and water for at least 20 seconds
  • Keep hand sanitizer as backup when soap isn't available
  • Clean under nails where bacteria can hide

Product-Specific Hygiene

Disposable Products

  • Wrap used products in toilet paper or wrapper
  • Dispose in sanitary bins, never flush
  • Change more frequently in hot weather
  • Avoid products with heavy fragrances

Reusable Products

  • Rinse cups with cold water first, then hot
  • Sterilize cups between cycles
  • Wash cloth pads in cold water first
  • Air dry reusable products when possible

Afternoon

Another product change if needed. Stay hydrated throughout the day.

Evening

Take a relaxing shower or bath. Use this time for self-care.

Bedtime

Use overnight products or higher absorbency for uninterrupted sleep.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I swim during my period?

Yes! Swimming is perfectly safe during your period. Use a tampon or menstrual cup for swimming. The water pressure actually reduces flow while you're in the water.

How do I manage odor during my period?

Change products regularly, maintain good hygiene, wear breathable fabrics, and avoid scented products. Remember, a slight metallic smell is normal - strong odors may indicate an infection.

Is it safe to use public restrooms during my period?

Yes, with proper precautions. Always wash your hands, use toilet seat covers or tissue, and carry your own supplies. Many restrooms have sanitary disposal bins.

What if I can't change my product on time?

While not ideal, occasional delays happen. Change as soon as possible, clean thoroughly, and watch for any signs of irritation or infection. Set reminders if you often forget.
