Understanding Your Menstrual Cycle

A comprehensive guide to the phases, symptoms, and tracking methods

What is the Menstrual Cycle?

The menstrual cycle is a natural process that prepares your body for pregnancy each month. Understanding your cycle helps you better manage your health and recognize what's normal for you.

Average Cycle Length

A typical menstrual cycle lasts between 21-35 days, with 28 days being the average. Every person's cycle is unique, and variations are completely normal.

1. Menstrual Phase

Duration: 3-7 days

This is when you have your period. The lining of your uterus (endometrium) sheds through your vagina.

  • Bleeding and cramping
  • Fatigue and low energy
  • Mood changes
  • Headaches or migraines

2. Follicular Phase

Duration: 13-14 days

Your body prepares for ovulation. Follicles in your ovaries mature and estrogen levels rise.

  • Increased energy
  • Better mood
  • Clearer skin
  • Higher libido

3. Ovulation Phase

Duration: 24-48 hours

A mature egg is released from the ovary. This is your most fertile time.

  • Slight temperature rise
  • Clear, stretchy discharge
  • Mild pelvic pain
  • Increased sex drive

4. Luteal Phase

Duration: 14 days

After ovulation, progesterone rises to maintain the uterine lining. If pregnancy doesn't occur, hormone levels drop.

  • PMS symptoms
  • Breast tenderness
  • Bloating
  • Mood swings

Common Symptoms Throughout Your Cycle

Physical Symptoms

  • Cramps and abdominal pain
  • Breast tenderness
  • Headaches
  • Bloating
  • Acne breakouts
  • Changes in appetite

Emotional Symptoms

  • Mood swings
  • Irritability
  • Anxiety
  • Depression
  • Difficulty concentrating
  • Changes in libido

When to See a Doctor

  • Periods lasting more than 7 days
  • Very heavy bleeding
  • Severe pain
  • Irregular cycles
  • Bleeding between periods
  • No period for 3+ months

How to Track Your Cycle

Digital Tracking

Use period tracking apps to log symptoms, predict cycles, and identify patterns. Popular apps include:

  • Clue
  • Flo
  • Period Tracker
  • Natural Cycles

Manual Tracking

Keep a journal or calendar to track:

  • Start and end dates
  • Flow intensity
  • Symptoms
  • Mood changes
  • Physical activity
